Points of interest

5 best places in the city

  1. Ibirapuera Park

    2. The most visited park in South America with a size of 390 acres.

  2. Batman's Alley

    3. Narrow streets that intersect, where street artists conquered their spaces. There are several 3D murals and colorful and painted artistic manifestations on the walls to take interesting photos.

  3. Aclimação Park

    4. The park has only 112,000 m² and was opened in 1939. Taking a walk or a bike ride around the lake is enough to get to know the place well.

  4. Paulista Avenue

    5. Paulista Avenue is one of the main tourist attractions in São Paulo. Along the 2.8 km avenue, there are several historic buildings as well as department stores.

  5. Liberdade neighborhood

    6. The stronghold of the Japanese community in São Paulo. It is very close to the historic center.

Points of attention

Things to know about the city

  • The people of São Paulo has already elected a rhinoceros for city counselor in 1959.
  • São Paulo is the second city that consumes more pizza in the world, behind only New York: there are more than 700 pizzas per minute!
  • The city has a bakery for dogs and cats. The Dog Bakery is located in the district of Cambuci, and one of the bakery's most sought-after products is a bone-shaped cake.
  • São Paulo concentrates the largest number of Japanese, Lebanese, and Italians outside their respective countries of origin. The population of Japanese and their descendants in the capital alone is estimated at nearly 40,000.
  • São Paulo is considered the helicopter capital of the world. There are more than 410 heliports across the city.
